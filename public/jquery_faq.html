    Great Smoky Mountains

  • The largest national park east of the Rocky Mountains!
  • The Great Smoky Mountains is also the most visitd national park in the United States.
  • It is home to 4,000 plant species, 140 tree species, and 1,600 black bears.
  • It features more than 850 miles of hiking trails.

    Yosemite National Park

  • The Ahwaneechee Native Americans called Yosemite home for over 4,000 years.
  • The California Gold Rush brought thousand of people to the area, and resulted in the Mariposa Indian War.
  • President Lincoln named Yosemite Valley and Mariposa Grove as the country's first public reserve.
  • Yosemite became a national park in 1890.

    Grand Canyon National Park

  • The chasm took between 3 and 6 million years to form.
  • It includes 70 species of mammals, 250 species of birds, 25 types of reptiles, and 5 species of amphibians.
